Socratic Revision Partner

Act as a Socratic revision partner. Ask me one short question at a time about [topic], using [my notes or learning outcomes] as context. Wait for my answer, identify one gap kindly, then ask the next question. Do not reveal a full answer until I have attempted it.

Customise: [topic], [notes or learning outcomes], [difficulty].

Use case: Active recall after reviewing a topic.

Follow-up: “Summarise my three weakest areas and suggest what I should revisit first.”

Reminder: Check explanations against your course materials.

Compare Research Sources

Compare [source A], [source B] and [source C]. For each, identify the main claim, evidence, method, limitations and publication context. Show agreement and disagreement in a table. Do not invent missing information; mark it as unknown.

Customise: Sources, research question and comparison criteria.

Use case: Organising reading before writing your own synthesis.

Follow-up: “Which claims must I verify in the original sources?”

Reminder: Read and cite the originals, not the generated comparison.

Structure Feedback Without Rewriting

Review my draft against [assignment question or purpose]. Identify the strongest point, the biggest gap and three improvements to structure or clarity. Explain what I should change, but do not rewrite the draft or imitate a different voice.

Customise: Draft, task, audience and marking criteria.

Use case: Developmental feedback on your own writing.

Follow-up: “Turn the improvements into a revision checklist without adding new claims.”

Reminder: Retain authorship and follow assessment guidance.

Explain This Error

Explain this [language or tool] error in plain English: [exact error]. Using only [small relevant code sample], identify the smallest likely fix, explain why it works and suggest one test. Do not rewrite unrelated code.

Customise: Language, exact error, code and expected behaviour.

Use case: Understanding a debugging step.

Follow-up: “What assumption could make this fix wrong?”

Reminder: Remove secrets and test every suggestion yourself.

Tailor Interview Preparation

Using this [role description] and my [experience notes], create six realistic interview questions. Ask one at a time, wait for my answer, then give feedback on evidence and clarity. Do not invent experience I have not supplied.

Customise: Role description, experience and interview format.

Use case: Practising evidence-based answers.

Follow-up: “Which answer needs a stronger example from my real experience?”

Reminder: Keep examples truthful and in your own words.

Plan a Realistic Study Week

Help me plan a realistic week around [deadlines], [fixed commitments] and [available hours]. Break work into small tasks, include breaks and contingency time, and flag anything unrealistic. Ask before assuming priorities.

Customise: Deadlines, commitments, available time and priorities.

Use case: Creating an adjustable weekly plan.

Follow-up: “What should I move if one task takes twice as long?”

Reminder: Review the plan daily and protect rest time.
