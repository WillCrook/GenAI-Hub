Project details

AI Study Coach Prototype

The problem

Priya wanted active-recall practice that adapted to confidence without replacing lectures, reading or tutor feedback.

The approach

She designed a small question loop, defined boundaries and tested it against topics she already understood.

How GenAI was used

The assistant generated one question at a time and suggested possible gaps. Priya wrote the system rules, supplied the subject context and decided what counted as useful feedback.

The final outcome

A prototype that records confidence and produces a student-reviewed list of topics to revisit.

Challenges

Early versions revealed answers too quickly and occasionally introduced material outside the unit.

What the student learnt

Smaller prompts, explicit source boundaries and repeated testing mattered more than adding features.

Responsible-use reflection

The coach is a revision aid, not an authority. Priya checks explanations against course material and does not upload personal or restricted data.

Lab Notes to Action Plan

The student designed a fixed template for decisions, actions, owners and uncertainties. AI proposes a structure; the research team confirms the record.

Responsible Research Mapper

Sources are selected and read by the student. AI helps expose relationships and unanswered questions, while the final synthesis remains the student’s work.

Placement Brief Builder

Generated questions help reveal missing constraints. The student verifies workplace details and writes the submitted brief.

Inclusive Caption Review

Automated flags prioritise manual review; students make every correction and consult speakers where meaning is uncertain.

Sustainable Design Idea Log

The student documents prompts, outputs, rejected concepts and design reasoning so AI-assisted exploration is transparent and critically reviewed.
