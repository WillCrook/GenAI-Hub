Not sure which lane?

Check your assessment brief first. It should tell you whether the assessment is Open Lane or Closed Lane, and whether you need to acknowledge any AI use.

How to check your assessment brief
Student responsibility

You're in the driving seat

AI can support your learning, but you remain responsible for the work you submit.

 

You make the decisions

Use your judgement. Do not let an AI tool decide what your answer should be.

 

You check the accuracy

AI can be wrong. Check facts, references, calculations and claims before you rely on them.

 

You follow academic integrity

Follow the rules for the assessment and be honest about how work was produced.

 

You submit your own work

Your submission must show your own understanding, choices and effort.

Open Lane

AI may be allowed

In an Open Lane assessment, GenAI use is optional or integral, depending on the task.

What this can include

Open Lane assessments may include coursework, reports, projects and open-book tasks. Your brief might allow AI for idea generation, planning, feedback, coding support, data exploration or another specific purpose.

Before using AI

Follow the assessment brief and acknowledge AI use where required. If the brief limits how AI can be used, those limits apply.

Closed Lane

AI must not be used

In a Closed Lane assessment, GenAI tools are not permitted.

What this can include

Closed Lane assessments may include invigilated exams, in-person tests and some practical assessments where AI would interfere with what is being assessed.

Important

Using AI in a Closed Lane assessment may breach academic integrity rules. If you are unsure, ask before you use it.

Before you submit

Assessment brief checklist

  Have I checked the assessment brief?
  Do I know which lane applies?
  Have I followed the AI-use instructions?
  Have I checked accuracy and sources?
  Have I acknowledged AI use if required?
